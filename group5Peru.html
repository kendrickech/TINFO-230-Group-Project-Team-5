A PERUVIAN'S GUIDE TO PERU

If you're searching for a destination that boasts stunning sandy beaches, towering mountains, and lush rainforests, look no further than Peru. This captivating country offers a wealth of experiences, from the history-filled ruins of Machu Picchu to the bustling streets of Lima, ensuring there's something for everyone to enjoy.

History

Peru's rich history has been shaped by numerous civilizations and significant events. The region was home to ancient cultures, such as the Norte Chico civilization and the Nazca. However, the most prominent and well-known of these civilizations was the Inca Empire, which emerged in the 15th century. It was the largest empire in pre-Columbian America and has gained global attention due to its advanced agricultural practices and the scenic ruins of Machu Picchu.

In 1532, conquistadors from Spain, led by Francisco Pizarro, conquered the Inca Empire, leading to centuries of colonial rule. During this time period, indigenous and Spanish cultures would blend together, significantly influencing Peru's language, religion, and traditions. Peru gained independence from Spanish rule on July 28th, 1821. After gaining independence, Peru faced political instability and territorial conflicts, but has since evolved into a vibrant nation known for its cultural heritage, natural beauty, and flavorful cuisine. Today, Peru celebrates its rich history while embracing modernity, making it a fascinating destination for travelers.

Places to Visit

Machu Picchu

Built in the 15th century, Machu Picchu is often referred to as the “Lost City of the Incas” and is one of the most visited historical sites of the world. It was built under the reign of Emperor Pachacuti around 1450 and is believed to have been abandoned by the Incas around the time the Spanish began taking over. The site was not rediscovered until 1911 by American historian Hiram Bingham. Since then, it has been declared a Peruvian Historical Sanctuary and allows tourists to see a glimpse of ancient Incan architecture.

The best town to stay in to visit Machu Picchu is Aguas Calientes. The nearest airport is located in Cusco, which is a 3-4 hour train ride away from Aguas Calientes. Once in Aguas Calientes, you can reach Machu Picchu by either bus or foot. The trek to Machu Picchu from Aguas Calientes takes about 2-3 hours and is generally considered to be a difficult trail, but it is worth it if you have the extra time and are up for the challenge.

Lima

Lima is the capital city of Peru and likely the first place you will be after flying into the country as it is home to Peru’s largest international airport. While Machu Picchu is heavily centered around Peru’s indigenous culture, Lima is more influenced by Spanish culture, which is easily seen throughout the city. Located in downtown Lima, there are multiple breathtaking cathedrals, all free or extremely cheap to enter. The Basilica and Convent of San Francisco has catacombs that can be toured for 15 soles (about $4 USD).

Besides the cathedrals, Lima has a spectacular food scene. Central, a restaurant in Lima, took the #1 spot on The World’s Best Restaurant 2023 list, sponsored by S.Pellegrino and Acqua Panna; however, there is no need to spend vast amounts of money at an upscale restaurant to enjoy the delicious food Peru has to offer. There are numerous local restaurants scattered across Lima that offer fresh, home-cooked meals for cheap. You can also find street vendors selling things like anticuchos (marinated and grilled cow heart served on skewers) and picarones (a kind of Peruvian doughnut often eaten alongside anticuchos). For shopping, visit Lima’s shopping centers, such as Plaza San Miguel, Real Plaza Salaverry, or the beautiful Larcomar, which provides a stunning view of Lima’s coast.

Punta Sal

Punta Sal is a stunning coastal resort destination located in northern Peru, known for its warm, sunny beaches year-round. Its clear waters and rich marine biodiversity make it ideal for snorkeling, diving, and fishing. The area provides a relaxing atmosphere and is home to many luxurious resorts that can be considered budget-friendly compared to other countries due to the conversion rate from soles to USD. Punta Sal is ideal for those in search of a peaceful retreat while on a budget.

Iquitos

Iquitos is a vibrant city located in the heart of the Peruvian Amazon, surrounded by lush rainforests and the Amazon River. Iquitos is a popular starting point for eco-tourism adventures due to the endless opportunities for jungle treks, wildlife watching, and river excursions. With the Amazon rainforest considered as one of the most biodiverse areas in the world, it is the perfect place to explore nature and encounter unique experiences. To get to Iquitos, you can either fly from the Jorge Chavez International Airport in Lima into Iquitos, which takes approximately 1.5 hours, or if you’d like to be more adventurous, you can reach Iquitos by boat. To get there by boat, you will start your journey at either Pucallpa or Yurimaguas. Depending on where you start, the boat ride can take anywhere from 3 to 7 days. Boat rides are typically hosted by cargo ships with hammocks for passengers to sleep in.

General Tips
  • Although many larger stores accept international credit/debit cards, it is best to use the money services within the airport to take out cash in soles.

  • If your phone plan does not include international service, get an esim card in order to use your phone without Wi-Fi.

  • If possible, learn basic phrases in Spanish to help you get around. Many people in tourist- heavy places know some English, but knowing a few common phrases in Spanish can help a lot.

  • Most drinks do not come on ice. You have to request it.

  • Carbonated water is extremely popular there, so look for "sin gas" on water bottles if you want uncarbonated water.

  • Bring medicine if you have a sensitive stomach, especially if you are planning on eating at smaller local restaurants.

Safety

Safety for tourists in Peru can vary by region. In areas with heavy tourism, there is usually a strong police presence which makes it safer for tourists to roam fairly freely. However, it is crucial to be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas, such as downtown Lima, to avoid falling victim to petty crimes, such as pickpocketing. Try to not carry big bills. As a tourist, it is best to avoid public transportation as it poses additional risks. Make sure to call a registered taxi service for transportation since anyone is able to put a taxi sign on their car. While in the taxi it is best to have all your documents secure and your valuable out of sight. Attempting to rent a car, especially in Lima, is highly discouraged as traffic laws are not heavily enforced there, making driving extremely difficult if you are not accustomed to the traffic there.

In more rural or less-traveled regions, it is recommended to travel with a guide, especially if you do not speak Spanish.
