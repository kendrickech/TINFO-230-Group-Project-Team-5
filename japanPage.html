Your Guide to Japan

In Japan, traditional and modern life blend captivatingly. There are many exciting cities and regions to explore during your visit! Tokyo is a fast-paced, energetic city with neon-lit streets, secret temples, and countless culinary explorations. You can be in the bustling center of Shibuya one minute, then receiving a fortune at a distinguished temple the next.

With its beautiful temples, bamboo forests, and Gion's alleyways dotted with geishas, Kyoto slows down time. It's like entering a different era, rich in beauty and history.

Next is Japan's culinary capital, Osaka. Dotonbori is a memorable place because of its vibrant lights, delicious takoyaki, and welcoming atmosphere. There's never a dull moment in the city â€” something exciting is always going on!

Each location has an own appeal, and when combined, they make Japan an unforgettable, stunning, and thrilling travel destination.

Tokyo

Tokyo is an incredible city witha mix of modern and traditional attractions, along with amazing food. Here are some popular attractions of the city:

Cultural & Historical Attractions

  • Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa) - Tokyo's oldest temple with a bustling market street.
  • Meiji Shrine (Harajuku) - A peaceful Shinto shrine surrounded by nature.
  • Imperial Palace - Beautiful gardens and home of Japan's Emperor.

Modern & Futuristic Attractions

  • Shibuya Crossing - The world's busiest pedestrian crossing.
  • Tokyo Skytree - A tall tower with stunning city views.
  • Akihabara - The center of anime, manga, and tech culture.

Food Experiences

  • Tsukiji Outer Market - Famous for fresh seafood and sushi.
  • Izakayas in Shinjuku or Shibuya - Traditional Japanese pub experiences.
  • Ramen at Ichiran or Afuri - Incredibly popular ramen spots.

Kyoto

Kyoto, the cultural heart of Japan, is known for its stunning temples, traditional districts, and breathtaking natural scenery. Here are some highlights of visiting Kyoto:

Cultural & Historical Attractions

  • Fushimi Inari Shrine - Famous for its thousands of red torii gates.
  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) - A stunning gold-leaf-covered Zen temple.
  • Kiyomizu-dera - A historic temple with breathtaking views of Kyoto.

Traditional Districts

  • Gion - Kyoto's famous geisha district with traditional tea houses.
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A magical bamboo forest and scenic area.
  • Pontocho Alley - A charming street filled with traditional restaurants and bars.

Food Experiences

  • Nishiki Market - A bustling market offering Kyoto's best street food.
  • Kaiseki Dining - Traditional multi-course Japanese cuisine.
  • Matcha in Uji - The best place to experience authentic Japanese green tea.

Osaka

Osaka, Japan's vibrant and food-loving city, offers a mix of historical landmarks, modern entertainment, and some of the best street food in the country. Here are some highlights of visiting Osaka:

Cultural & Historical Attractions

  • Osaka Castle - A stunning historic castle surrounded by beautiful gardens.
  • Shitenno-ji Temple - Japan's oldest officially recognized Buddhist temple.
  • Sumiyoshi Taisha - A unique Shinto shrine with beautiful arched bridges.

Entertainment & Modern Attractions

  • Dotonbori - Famous for neon lights, giant billboards, and street food.
  • Shinsaibashi Shopping Street - A paradise for shoppers.
  • Umeda Sky Building - Offers an incredible panoramic city view.
  • Osaka Aquarium (Kaiyukan) - One of the world's largest aquariums.

Food Experiences

  • Kuromon Ichiba Market - A lively market with fresh seafood and local street food.
  • Takoyaki & Okonomiyaki - Osaka's famous octopus balls and savory pancakes.
  • Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower - A retro district known for kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers).

Important information

  • Language: Japanese (Google Translate helps)
  • Currency: Yen (¥) / Conversion rate: (1 USD : ~149 JPY)
  • Transportation: Trains (Bullet Train, Subways, Local Trains), Buses, Taxis & Ride-Sharing
  • Accomodation: Hotels, Ryokan (Traditional Japanese Inns), Capsule Hotels, Guesthouses & Hotels
  • Safety: Japan is very clean safe with low crime rates, but be mindful of your belongings at all times

Travel Tips

  • Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass): Consider getting this pass to save money on bullet trains if planning to travel between cities.
  • IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo): Useful card you can load money onto for public transportation and small purchases at stores or vending machines.
  • Bowing: Common form of greeting and respect in Japan.
  • Shoes off indoors: Remove shoes before entering houses, inns, and sometimes restaurants.
  • No Tipping: Tipping is not expected and is even considered rude sometimes.
  • Keep Cash: Many places prefer cach over credit cards, though digital payments are becoming more popular.
  • Silence in Public Transportation: Public transportation is very quiet; avoid talking loudly or making phone calls.
  • Google Translate & Offline Maps: Very useful for communication and in case you are lost.
  • Tattoos: Tattoos can still be stigmatized in certain places in Japan so be wary and do research beforehand.
